Transparency

How Product.ai makes money without compromising what it tells you

Product.ai is free for everyone. When you buy through a partner link, a share of the sale supports our mission. Our recommendations never change based on who pays us. Here's how that works - and how you can verify it.

The model

Product.ai earns revenue through affiliate partnerships with retailers. When you purchase a product through one of our links, the retailer pays us a commission. You pay nothing extra - the price is identical whether you buy through us or go directly.

This is the same model we've operated at SimplyCodes for 15 years across 400,000+ stores. It's not new. It's not experimental. It works because our incentives are aligned with yours: if we give you bad advice, you don't come back.

Mission
Revenue keeps Product.ai free for everyone

The arrow only goes one direction. Revenue follows truth - truth never follows revenue. We call this the Fiduciary Diode: the information we give you produces commerce, and commerce produces revenue. The revenue never flows backward to influence the information.

What the dot means

When you see a small violet dot next to a retailer name in Product.ai, it means that retailer is one of our partners. If you purchase through that link, Product.ai earns a commission.

Retailers without a dot are not partners. We recommend them anyway when they have the best price or the best product. We cover over 400,000 stores - the majority are not partners. Our coverage is identical regardless of partnership status.

What we will always do

Recommend the best product, regardless of partnership

If the best product is sold by a retailer we don't partner with, we recommend it anyway and earn nothing.

Show the best price, regardless of who profits

If a non-partner retailer has the lowest price, it appears first. We never reorder results to favor partners.

Tell you not to buy something when that's the honest answer

Our Kill Shot verdicts exist specifically for this. No affiliate-funded site would tell you not to buy a $2,499 product. We do.

Keep 100% of capabilities free, permanently

No subscription. No premium tier. No "upgrade for the full verdict." Every feature, every user, free.

What we will never do

Let commission rates influence recommendations

Some retailers pay higher commissions than others. This data never enters our recommendation system.

Accept payment from brands for favorable coverage

No sponsored verdicts. No paid placements. No "featured" products. Our recommendations are editorial, not commercial.

Sell your data or preferences

Your shopping preferences, product research, and purchase history are yours. We use them to improve your experience. We never sell them.

Questions we hear

If most retailers are partners, doesn't that mean most links earn you money anyway?
Many large retailers have affiliate programs, yes. But many don't - especially direct-to-consumer brands, niche retailers, and international sellers. We cover all of them equally. The test isn't whether we earn money on most purchases. The test is whether we'd recommend a non-partner product over a partner product when the non-partner product is better. We do. Every time.
How is this different from Wirecutter or other review sites that earn affiliate revenue?
Two differences. First, our recommendations are generated by an AI system trained on verified evidence, not written by editors who might unconsciously favor reviewable products. Second, our Verifiable Trust Ledger makes our independence cryptographically provable, not just promised. Wirecutter says their editorial and commerce teams don't talk. We make it mathematically verifiable that our recommendations don't correlate with partnerships.
Why not charge a subscription instead? Wouldn't that be cleaner?
We considered it seriously. Subscriptions create a different problem: the best product advice becomes available only to people who can afford it. We think honest product intelligence should be free for everyone. The affiliate model lets us do that while keeping our incentives aligned - we earn when you make a purchase you're happy with, not when you pay a monthly fee regardless.
What if your business grows and the pressure to optimize revenue increases?
This is the right question. Our answer has three parts. First, we've resisted this pressure for 15 years as a bootstrapped company with zero outside investors - there's no board pushing for quarterly growth. Second, the Verifiable Trust Ledger creates a permanent, public record that would expose any drift. Third, we publish quarterly transparency reports so the trend is visible over time. If we ever compromise, the evidence will be public.
