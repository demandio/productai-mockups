BIOAVAILABILITY FORGED
Supplement form determines bioavailability more than dosage. Magnesium glycinate absorbs at 2.3x the rate of magnesium oxide despite identical label claims.
The chelation bond in glycinate protects the mineral through gastric acid (pH 1.5-3.5), enabling intestinal absorption. Oxide dissociates in the stomach, converting to insoluble hydroxide. Bioavailability: glycinate 80% vs oxide 4%.
SUPP-BIO-001 - Forged Feb 2026 - 2 challenges resolved, axiom stands
▶ Evidence
S
ConsumerLab 2024 Magnesium Report: Tested 40 supplements. Oxide: 3-5% urinary recovery. Chelated forms: 18-24%.consumerlab.com/reviews/magnesium/
S
Firoz & Graber (2001), J Am Coll Nutr: Crossover study comparing four magnesium preparations. Chelated forms showed highest fractional absorption.doi: 10.1080/07315724.2001.10719064
T
Adversarial verification: Three independent research agents converged on the bioavailability differential. ChatGPT baseline recommended oxide citing "highest elemental magnesium" - failed to distinguish label dose from absorbed dose.
ECONOMICS UNDER REVIEW

SAFETY FORGED
Supplements cause 23,000 ER visits per year in the US. Risk concentrates in weight loss (25%), energy/bodybuilding (20%), and heart health (15%).

Third-party testing reveals 89% of supplement performance labels contain inaccurate claims. Products marketed as "clinically dosed" frequently contain 40-60% of the stated active ingredient.
