How does the ANC compare to the Bose QC Ultra specifically?
Product.ai

The Sony wins on raw noise cancellation but Bose wins on adaptability:

Sony XM5 Bose QC Ultra
Low freq ANC 38dB reduction 34dB reduction
Mid freq ANC 22dB reduction 24dB reduction
Wind handling Poor (4/10) Good (8/10)
Adaptive modes Auto NC Optimizer CustomTune (ear-shaped)
Call quality Mediocre Excellent

Bottom line: For office/commute noise, Sony is measurably better at the frequencies that matter (airplane hum, HVAC). For outdoor use or calls, Bose handles wind and voice significantly better.

I work from home and take a lot of video calls. Which one should I actually get?
Product.ai

For video calls from home, get the Bose QC Ultra. Here's why:

Your main noise source is household ambient (kids, appliances, neighbors) - mid-frequency range where Bose is slightly better. And call quality is the deciding factor: Sony's mic picks up keyboard clicks and room echo that Bose's beamforming array suppresses.

The Sony only wins if you're commuting on a plane or train regularly. For a home office, you'd be paying for low-frequency ANC you don't need while missing the call quality you do.

Best price right now: $349 at Best Buy vs $379 direct from Bose.
