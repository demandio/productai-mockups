Key axioms

These are the verified physics that power Product.ai's supplement recommendations. Each axiom has been forged through adversarial research and endorsed by domain experts. Click any axiom to see its full evidence chain.

BIOAVAILABILITY FORGED
Supplement form determines bioavailability more than dosage. Magnesium glycinate absorbs at 2.3x the rate of magnesium oxide despite identical label claims.
The chelation bond in glycinate forms protects the mineral through gastric acid (pH 1.5-3.5), enabling intestinal absorption. Oxide forms dissociate in the stomach, converting to insoluble hydroxide at intestinal pH. Bioavailability: glycinate 80% vs oxide 4%.

LABEL ACCURACY FORGED
Third-party testing reveals 89% of supplement performance labels contain inaccurate claims. Products marketed as "clinically dosed" frequently contain 40-60% of the stated active ingredient.
No FDA pre-market approval required for supplements (DSHEA 1994). GMP compliance is self-certified. Third-party verification (USP, NSF, ConsumerLab) is voluntary and expensive ($15-50K per SKU). Only ~3% of supplement SKUs carry independent verification.

SAFETY FORGED
Supplements cause 23,000 ER visits per year in the US. The risk concentrates in three categories: weight loss (25%), energy/bodybuilding (20%), and heart health supplements (15%).
Unlike pharmaceuticals, supplements require no adverse event reporting system until 2007 (and current reporting captures estimated 1-10% of events). Contamination with undeclared pharmaceutical ingredients (sildenafil, sibutramine, synthetic steroids) accounts for 40% of ER cases.

ECONOMICS UNDER REVIEW
DTC supplement brands command 30-50% affiliate commissions vs 1-3% on Amazon, creating a structural incentive for review sites to recommend DTC brands regardless of product quality.
Amazon affiliate rate for health/personal care: 1%. Typical DTC supplement affiliate: 30-50% of first purchase + 10-20% recurring. A $60 DTC supplement generates $18-30 affiliate commission vs $0.60 on Amazon for an identical product.

INTERACTION RISK FORGED
Supplement-drug interactions are clinically significant and systematically under-warned. St. John's Wort alone interacts with 50+ prescription medications including oral contraceptives, SSRIs, and blood thinners.
CYP3A4 enzyme induction by hyperforin (St. John's Wort active compound) accelerates metabolism of co-administered drugs by 200-400%. This reduces effective drug concentration below therapeutic threshold. Effect persists 2 weeks after discontinuation.

VERIFICATION FORGED
Three third-party verification bodies provide meaningful quality signals: USP (gold standard, pharmaceutical-grade testing), NSF International (sports-focused, banned substance screening), and ConsumerLab (broadest coverage, ~700 products/year).
USP verification requires identity, strength, purity, and dissolution testing. NSF Certified for Sport tests for 270+ banned substances. ConsumerLab purchases products retail (unannounced) vs. manufacturer-submitted samples, reducing selection bias. All three are voluntary and manufacturer-funded, creating survivorship bias in the "verified" pool.
