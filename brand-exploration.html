5 claims we verified this week
1
"Retinol causes purging" -- Mostly True
Initial breakouts from retinoid use reflect accelerated cell turnover. 78% of dermatologists confirm this mechanism. But: duration beyond 8 weeks is NOT purging.
2
"Hyaluronic acid hydrates skin" -- Conditional
True in humid climates. In dry environments, HA can pull moisture FROM skin. Molecular weight matters: low-MW penetrates, high-MW sits on surface.
3
"This serum reverses aging" -- False
No topical reverses chronological aging. The peptide in question showed 4% wrinkle depth reduction in one in-vitro study. The brand extrapolated.

Check everything. Glow with confidence.
Community-native language. "Glow" is the aspirational state every skincare enthusiast is chasing. "Check" implies verification -- what Product.ai actually does. The "Powered by" Connection

All three candidates share the same trust architecture underneath. The category brand owns the relationship with the user. Product.ai owns the verification methodology. When users encounter a second category product (e.g., supplements), they see a different brand with a familiar "powered by Product.ai" trust signal -- like how Google Maps and YouTube are different products with the same trust substrate.
